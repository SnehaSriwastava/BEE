1. TAWANG, ARUNACHAL PRADESH.

TAWANG.

/Tah • Wahng./
noun.

Tawang, where the mountains touch the sky, is a Himalayan haven draped in clouds and ancient whispers. This mystical town, crowned by the majestic Tawang Monastery, is a sanctuary of peace, where serene lakes reflect the grandeur of snow-clad peaks. In Tawang, every moment is a brushstroke on the canvas of nature's most poetic masterpiece.

NUMBER OF VISITORS : 30,000 - 40,000

2. SHETLAND ISLANDS, SCOTLAND.

SHETLAND ISLAND.

/shet • luhnd/
noun.

Shetland, where ancient Norse legends meet the untamed beauty of Scotland, offers a landscape sculpted by winds and waves, rich with hidden tales. Here, nature reigns supreme, with puffins, seals, and wild ponies sharing the rugged coastlines and serene lochs. It’s a place where time slows, inviting explorers to uncover the soul of the North Atlantic's wild frontier.

3. TUVALU, OCEANIA.

TUVALU.

/too • vuh • loo/
noun.

Tuvalu, a jewel of the Pacific, glimmers with pristine atolls and azure waters, where the horizon blends seamlessly with the sky. Here, life dances to the rhythm of the ocean, and every tide tells a story of resilience and hope. As the sea encroaches, Tuvalu stands as a living testament to the delicate balance between nature's beauty and its profound fragility.

SÃO TOMÉ & PRÍNCIPE.

/Sow • Tom-eh • Preen-si-peh/
noun.

São Tomé and Príncipe, where emerald rainforests meet sapphire seas, is a treasure chest of natural wonder. This idyllic archipelago is a canvas of vibrant landscapes and rich culture, inviting explorers to discover its captivating and timeless allure.
